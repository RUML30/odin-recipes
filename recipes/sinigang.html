Sinigang

Description

Sinigang is a beloved Filipino dish known for its bold and tangy flavors. This traditional sour soup is a staple in Filipino cuisine and is often made with a variety of ingredients, including pork, shrimp, or fish. The hallmark of a great Sinigang recipe is the use of tamarind or other souring agents, which impart a delightful sourness to the broth. The combination of tender meat or seafood, an array of vegetables such as kangkong (water spinach), eggplant, and radish, creates a hearty and nutritious soup. Sinigang's distinct taste is both comforting and refreshing, making it a popular choice for family meals and gatherings. What makes Sinigang even more appealing is its versatility. There are countless regional variations and ingredient combinations, allowing you to adapt the recipe to your taste preferences. Whether you enjoy the rich flavors of pork belly in your Sinigang or prefer the lightness of shrimp or fish, this dish offers a delightful balance of savory and sour notes. Sinigang is not just a meal but a comforting taste of Filipino heritage, embodying the warmth of home-cooked dishes that bring families and friends together around the dining table.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs pork belly
  • 1 bunch spinach
  • 3 tablespoons fish sauce
  • 12 pieces string beans
  • 2 pieces tomato
  • 3 pieces chili
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 2 quarts water
  • 1 piece onion
  • 2 pieces taro
  • 1 pack sinigang mix

Steps

  1. Heat the pot and put-in the cooking oil
  2. Saut√© the onion until its layers separate
  3. Add the pork belly and cook until outer part turns light brown
  4. Put-in the fish sauce and mix with the ingredients
  5. Pour the water and bring to a boil
  6. Add the taro and tomatoes then simmer for 40 minutes or until pork is tender
  7. Put-in the sinigang mix and chili
  8. Add the string beans (and other vegetables if there are any) and simmer for 5 to 8 minutes
  9. Put-in the spinach, turn off the head, and cover the pot. Let the spinach cook using the remaining head in the pot
  10. Serve hot. Share and enjoy!
